<p><p>The camera battery market comprises primarily two types: NiMH (Nickel-Metal Hydride) and Lithium-Ion batteries. NiMH batteries are often more affordable and have a longer life cycle, making them suitable for entry-level cameras. However, they typically have lower energy density, resulting in shorter usage times. In contrast, Lithium-Ion batteries are favored for their lightweight design, higher energy capacity, and quick recharge capabilities, making them ideal for advanced and professional cameras, where performance and longevity are crucial.</p></p>
