<p><p>District Heating and Cooling (DHC) services involve centralized systems that provide heating and cooling to multiple buildings within a specific area. District Heating utilizes hot water or steam generated from a central source to distribute warmth, often sourced from renewable energy, waste heat, or cogeneration plants. Conversely, District Cooling employs chilled water systems to deliver cooling, typically produced through large-scale chillers. Both systems enhance energy efficiency, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and provide reliable climate control for urban populations.</p></p>
