<p><p>Methyl Cellulose (MC) and Hydroxypropyl Methylcellulose (HPMC) are cellulose derivatives widely used as thickening agents, emulsifiers, and film-forming agents in various industries. MC is a non-ionic compound derived from cellulose through methylation, while HPMC incorporates hydroxypropyl groups, enhancing its solubility and thermal gelation properties. These compounds are commonly found in food products, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and construction materials due to their biodegradability and non-toxic nature.</p><p>The Methyl Cellulose (MC) and Hydroxypropyl Methylcellulose (HPMC) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.3% during the forecast period. Innovations in product formulations and sustainable manufacturing practices are also shaping market dynamics, making MC and HPMC increasingly attractive to various industries seeking reliable, high-performance materials.
